Rustic Sourdough Bread recipe

Ingredients

  • 225g ripe and fed sourdough starter
  • 810g of Bread Flour
  • 90g of Whole Wheat Flour
  • 600g Lukewarm water
  • 18g Kosher Salt

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl combine all of the whole wheat flour, 710g of the bread flour, and 500g of lukewarm water. Mix until well-combined and no dry flour spots remain. This is called the autolyse and helps with gluten strength. Let rest covered for 30 minutes.
  2. To the bowl add the discard of your sourdough starter, about 225g. Add the remaining bread flour (100g), and water (100g), and salt. Mix well until well-combined and let rest covered for 30 minutes.
  3. To help with gluten formation, lift the dough up by the edge and fold over itself. Rotate the bowl 90 degrees and complete for each of the 4 edges of the dough. Let rest for 30 minutes covered.
  4. Repeat step 3 three additional times. Once completed, let rest for 4 hours.
  5. Remove dough from bowl and split into two equal masses. Shape the dough into rounds and place into bannetons. Cover the bannetons and let rest overnight in the fridge.
  6. The next day, begin preheating oven with dutch oven in main oven for one hour at 425℉
  7. Remove one of the loafs from the fridge and place on parchment paper. Using a sharp knife or bread lame, cut a long line over the top, or any other pattern you wish. Carefully remove the hot dutch oven and place the loaf in. Optionally spray the loaf with water to help with oven rise. Cover and let bake for 25 minutes.
  8. Take the lid of the dutch oven and drop the heat to 350℉. Bake for around 20 minutes or until a nice brown crust is formed.
  9. Remove loaf from oven and move over to baking rack. Let cool for over an hour to help preserve the loaf. Meanwhile, repeat steps 7 & 8 for second loaf.
